Home by Home Plan

Data-led planning for smarter building improvements

A solution helping housing providers plan, prioritise and deliver improvements more effectively — now embedded at the heart of Greenwich's £430 million Housing Capital Programme.

The challenge

Why housing providers need a better plan

The net zero journey for housing providers is one of the most complex technical, organisational and financial challenges many of them have ever faced — especially alongside existing pressures to provide safe and comfortable homes, rapid cost inflation and shrinking budgets.

The Home by Home Plan defines what improvement works will have to happen for each home, when, and how much they might cost — with data at the heart of every decision.

AI-powered prioritisation

We use AI to analyse tens or hundreds of thousands of repairs — giving a real-life understanding of each home in the portfolio

Integrated with EPC data, stock condition surveys, repairs history and archetype information.

How it works

A three-phase approach

1

Data

The plan starts with deep understanding of the housing stock — drawing on stock condition data, repair history, EPC ratings, compliance data and tenant satisfaction. We clean and validate datasets, then use AI to categorise repair records. Rules-based prioritisation produces a traffic-light system highlighting urgency of specific works across the portfolio.

2

Plan

Once priorities are set, we determine which projects need to happen at each home — combining works that create efficiencies when completed together, minimising disruption to residents. At estate scale, home-level works are coordinated with block-level requirements to create coherent programmes aligned with broader council priorities and budgets.

3

Deliver

The final phase manages data flow during delivery and beyond — an iterative, long-term process keeping analysis live and targets on track. IoT sensors provide real-time condition data. The plan is highly flexible and can be reprioritised to support short-term contractor mobilisation, medium-term budget requests, and long-term strategic decisions.

Real-world impact

Embedded in Greenwich's £430m Housing Capital Programme

The Royal Borough of Greenwich instructed DG Cities to complete an in-depth analysis of their entire housing stock — covering 21,000+ homes across 112 estates. The objective was to develop a prioritisation framework to identify homes with the greatest need and enable targeted, evidence-based capital investment.

The DG Cities Prioritisation Tool is now formally embedded in Greenwich's Housing Asset Management Strategy 2025–2030 as the methodology underpinning how the council allocates its entire Housing Capital Programme.
