Damp & Mould Intelligence Service

From reactive management to proactive risk intelligence

Data-driven identification, prevention and management of damp and mould in social housing — combining environmental monitoring, housing data integration and financial modelling.

The challenge

Why damp & mould intelligence matters

Damp and mould remain one of the most significant operational, financial and compliance challenges facing social landlords. Regulatory pressure, increasing reactive repair costs and limited visibility between inspections mean traditional approaches are often reactive and resource intensive.

Environmental monitoring enables landlords to move toward a proactive model — identifying risk earlier, targeting interventions more effectively, and linking damp risk to broader asset and retrofit strategies.

We transform sensor data into operational insight and strategic decision making — strengthening compliance, improving targeting of interventions and supporting long term asset performance.

What we deliver

Our service

Sensor Deployment & Strategy

We help landlords deploy environmental sensors where they will deliver the greatest impact, using asset data, archetypes and known risk indicators to identify priority homes and align deployment with retrofit programmes.

Data Integration & Dashboarding

We integrate environmental monitoring with repairs data, EPC ratings and property archetypes to create a unified view of risk — delivered through a clear prioritisation tool using platforms like Power BI.

Insights & Analysis

We turn environmental data into actionable insight — identifying high-risk homes early, analysing links between reactive spend and environmental conditions, and tracking impact of works over time.

Business Case & Financial Modelling

Robust financial modelling including lifecycle analysis, scenario modelling and comparisons between reactive and sensor-enabled approaches — quantifying avoided costs and ROI.

Process Transformation

Our Damp & Mould Intelligence Framework embeds monitoring into everyday operations — from identifying portfolio risk to targeted deployment, data integration and operational response.

Case study

Royal Borough of Greenwich

DG Cities partnered with the Royal Borough of Greenwich to design and deliver a data-led damp and mould monitoring pilot. 500 environmental sensors were installed across 200 council homes on 42 estates — with sensor data integrated with repairs records, property archetype data and retrofit programme information.

The project moved the council from a reactive, inspection-led model toward proactive risk identification — enabling risk scoring, intervention tracking and a robust business case for wider deployment.
